My friends call me Jabriel (JB). I'm on a mission to become both a master storyteller
and a skilled dragon-slayer.

I currently work as a [Game Engine Programmer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_engine). Much of my day-to-day focuses on performance optimization, system design, and tool development. On large-scale projects, I’ve delivered performance improvements in asset management, the rendering pipeline, and memory usage. I’m especially passionate about designing and implementing efficient, scalable systems that can support complex game worlds and truly immersive experiences.

Storytelling has always captivated me — whether it lives in the pages of a book, the frames of a film, or the interactive spaces of video games. I love using code to help shape compelling narratives and build adventures that pull players in and don’t let go.

Outside of games, I love [climbing](https://www.boulderbrighton.com/what-is-bouldering) and I’m an amateur fan of theoretical physics, with a particular fondness for Richard Feynman.
